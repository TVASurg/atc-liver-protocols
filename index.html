Welcome to the Ajmera Transplant Centre

The Ajmera Transplant Centre at University Health Network (UHN) is the largest and most comprehensive transplant program in Canada and ranks among the top five in North America. With internationally recognized excellence in kidney, liver, lung, heart, pancreas, and intestinal transplantation, the Centre is a hub for clinical innovation, research, and patient-centered care. Its strength lies in a deeply collaborative, multidisciplinary model that integrates surgeons, hepatologists, nephrologists, cardiologists, infectious disease specialists, nursing, allied health, and transplant coordinators. Patients from across Canada and around the world are referred to the Centre for complex transplant care, drawn by its superior surgical outcomes and groundbreaking research in immune tolerance, organ repair, and personalized medicine. As part of its vision outlined in the Transplant 2025 strategy, the Ajmera Transplant Centre continues to lead the transformation of transplant care through digital innovation, quality improvement, and education.

Welcome Residents and Fellows!

Welcome to your Liver Transplant Rotation at UHN’s Toronto General Hospital (TGH). This rotation provides exposure to one of Canada’s busiest and most advanced liver transplant programs, performing over 200 liver transplants annually. Approximately one-third of these are from living donors, with the remaining two-thirds from deceased donation. At any given time, over 230 patients are active on the waitlist. Since its inception, the program has completed more than 4,600 liver transplants, including over 1,300 living donor procedures. In addition to surgery, the program delivers comprehensive, lifelong post-transplant care.

Your rotation typically includes two weeks on inpatient service and two weeks in outpatient clinics, with one weekend of home call. This app is designed to support your experience by providing centralized access to key protocols, orientation materials, and clinical tools. Whether you’re managing new referrals, participating in transplant rounds, or following up with post-transplant patients, this resource will help guide your workflow and enhance your understanding of liver transplantation at UHN.
