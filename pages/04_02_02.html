Medications (Anti-rejection):

Prednisone:

Give Prednisone at the following schedule:

POD1 Methylprednisolone 200mg IV
POD2 Methylprednisolone 160mg IV
POD3 Methylprednisolone 120mg IV
POD4 Methylprednisolone 80mg IV
POD5 Methylprednisolone 40mg IV
POD6 and beyond

Oral prednisone 20mg daily → Continue for 2 weeks

  • Taper down to 15mg po daily for 2 weeks.
  • Taper down to 10mg po daily for 2 weeks.
  • Taper down to 5mg po daily for 2 weeks
  • Taper down to 5mg po every other day for 2 weeks then discontinue

Basilixmab (Simulect)

Indicated in the following:

  • Baseline creatinine above 150 (EGFR 30-60)
  • Patients on HD pre-transplant
  • Patients who are expected to not be able to receive appropriate tacrolimus dose on a timely fashion (in patients with delirium, fragile and older than 70).
  • Dose: 20mg IV on POD 0 → 20mg IV once on POD 4.
    • Can be given every 2 weeks if unable to get patient on appropriate immunosuppression

ATG (Alternative ot Basilixmad, given only if contraindication to Basilixmab)

  • Dose:
    • Low risk patients (ABO compatible or first transplant): ATG 3-5 mg/kg over 5 days
    • High risk patients (ABO incompatible or re-transplant): ATG 5-7 mg/kg over 5 days.

Tacrolimus (Prograf) – 1st line:

  • All patients should receive 1-2 mg PO bid on POD1
    • Consider 0.5mg bid if delirium, malnourished and patients with AKI or on HD
    • Consider 2-3mg bid if the patient is young or BMI above 35
    • Moderate dose Myfortic (180-360) patient should have dose reduced by 30%
    • High dose Myfortic (540-720) patient should have dose reduced by 50%.
    • Sublingual tacrolimus available (decrease dose by 20%)
  • Advagraf (once daily dosing regimen) can be given for patients with coverage (increase dose by 25%)

Target Tac Levels:

Cyclosporine (APO-cyclosporine) – 2nd line:

  • Indicated for patients with Tacrolimus allergy or unmanageable CNS side-effects from Tacrolimus
  • Dose: 50-100 mg po bid on POD1
    • Consider 50 mg bid if delirium, malnourished and patients with AKI or on HD
    • Consider 200 mg bid if the patient is young or BMI above 35
    • Moderate dose Myfortic (180-360) patient should have dose reduced by 30%
    • High dose Myfortic (540-720) patient should have dose reduced by 50%.  

Target C2 Levels:

Sirolimus (Rapamune) – 3rd Line:

  • Indicated for the following patients:
    • Contraindication from Tac or C2 (i.e., high creatinine, CNS side effects)
    • Private Coverage + Cancer treatment with liver transplant  
    • Prive Coverage + HCC RETREAT score >4
  • Dose: 2-5 mg po bid on POD1
    • DO NOT USE during first month post-transplant (Risk of hepatic artery stenosis)  
    • Moderate dose Myfortic (180-360) patient should have dose reduced by 30%
    • High dose Myfortic (540-720) patient should have dose reduced by 50%.  

Target Sirolimus Levels: 

Myfortic:

  • Indicated for the following patients:
    • Unable to reach target from Tac/C2 or present with mild CKD/CNS side effects from Tac/C2
    • PSC, PBC, AIH, re-transplant patients
    • High risk of rejection patients (CMV mismatch, young females)
  • Dose:  Start at 180 mg po bid on POD2-5 (dose 180/360/540/720 mg)
    • For NG Tube patients use Cellcept (dose at 250/500/750 mg)
    • IV Dose same as oral
    • Hold if following:
      • Active infection  
      • WBC < 2 → restart when > 3 and Hb above 80
      • Platelets < 40 → restart when > 50 and Hb above 80

Azanthioprine (Imuran) – Alternative to Myfortic:

  • Indicated for patients intolerant of myfortic (i.e., GI side effects or allergy)
  • TMPT testing must be done before use
  • Dose: 1-2mg/kg per day (25/50/75/100mg po daily)
    • IV Dose same as oral
    • Hold if following:
      • Active infection  
      • WBC < 2 → restart when > 3 and Hb above 80
      • Platelets < 40 → restart when > 60 and Hb above 80
  • Start 0.5mg/kg per day if WBC is 2-3 or platelets is 40-60.
