Neuroendocrine Liver Metastases:

Liver transplantation (LT) is a potential curative option for a highly selected subset of patients with liver-only metastases from well-differentiated neuroendocrine tumors (NETs). Given the variability in disease progression and limited prospective data, strict inclusion criteria have been proposed to optimize outcomes.

Eligibility Criteria for LT in NET Patients at UHN

  • Age: <65 years (flexible based on comorbidities and transplant candidacy)
  • Tumor Differentiation: Well-differentiated NETs (G1/G2) with Ki-67/MIB ≤10%
  • Primary Tumor: Must be resected ≥6 months prior to transplant and drain via the portal venous system
  • Disease Stability: Must demonstrate stable disease for ≥6 months; patients progressing on first-line therapy should undergo second-line treatment before transplant consideration
  • Disease Burden: No volume restriction, but liver disease must be unresectable
  • Metastasis Location: Strictly liver-only; no extrahepatic disease confirmed by Ga68-PET
  • Treatment History: No limit on number of systemic/locoregional treatment lines
  • Liver Transplant Type: Both living donor and deceased donor grafts accepted

Evaluation Approach

  • Prospective implementation of selection criteria
  • Comparison of outcomes with non-transplanted controls
  • Collaboration with SEER database for matched cohort analysis
