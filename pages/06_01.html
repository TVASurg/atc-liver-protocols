Transition of Pediatric Patients:

The transition of pediatric liver transplant (LT) recipients to adult care typically occurs between ages 17–18. This process is structured to ensure a smooth handover, empower the patient, and reduce parental reliance while maintaining continuity of care.

Long-Term Follow-up

  • Stable Patients: Annual follow-ups post-transition.
  • High-Risk/Compliance Issues: More frequent contact and tailored support.
    • High-Risk (20%):
      • Patients with limited social/family support or recent problematic healthcare behaviors.
      • Require more intensive social and clinical support.
    • Moderate-Risk (30%):
      • Patients still highly dependent on parents for health management.
      • Benefit from more frequent monitoring and follow-up.
  • Goal: Implement a government-supported program with dedicated social work and coordination for high-risk multi-organ transplant (MOT) patients.
