Immune Checkpoint Therapy:

Immune Checkpoint Inhibitor (ICI) Therapy (Prior to Liver Tx):

  • All patients with hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC) beyond the Milan criteria should be considered for ICI therapy for the following reasons:
    • High response rates observed with ICI in combination with other loco-regional treatments (LRT)
    • Reduced risk of post-transplant recurrence (due to more effective tumor down staging) as shown in current literature.
  • Patients on ICI before transplant should be listed on hold.  
  • Patients with exceptional responses to immuno-oncology (IO) based combinations can be considered as candidates for liver transplantation (downstaging)
  • Patients with vascular invasion should be included in the downstaging trial.
  • ICI should be stopped when exceptional MELD reaches 28 points for Blood Groups A, B, O (for AB dependent on the number of patients on the waitlist).  
  • Patient can be activated 6 weeks after stop of ICI.  
    • Physician should clarify on admission for transplant when the last dose of ICI was given.

Washout Period: If liver transplantation is done after downstaging therapy with ICI wait time should be minimally 6 weeks, best 12 weeks before transplant.

If transplant occurs <6 weeks after ICI:

  • Pre-transplant: Consider plasmapheresis (PLEX), IVIG
  • Induction: Simulect on day 0 and 4, IVIG on days 5 and 10
  • Start tacrolimus (target trough 8–10 ng/mL) pre-op
  • Myfortic 540 mg
  • Maintain prednisone 7.5 mg for 6 months post-op

Immune Checkpoint Inhibitor (ICI) Therapy (After Liver Tx):

  • HCC: Due to rejection risk, ICI should be considered after phase-3 evidence treatments (Sorafenib, Lenvatinib, Cabozantinib, Regorafenib, Folfox)
    • ICI use selectively in patients with expected high response rate:
      • Melanoma - up to 50% response rate
      • Non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC) – 20-45% response rate
      • Renal cell carcinoma (RCC)
      • Urothelial carcinoma
      • Microsatellite instability-high (MSI-H) or mismatch repair-deficient (dMMR) tumors – 30-50% response rate
  • Inform patients about increased risk of rejection (20-40%), possible organ loss/death
  • Avoid dual checkpoint inhibition  
  • Prednisone ≤10 mg is considered safe with ICI (does not impact effectiveness)
