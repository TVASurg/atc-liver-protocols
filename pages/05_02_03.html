When to suspect sepsis:

  • Post-op fever
  • Organ dysfunction or Suspected Infection
  • Positive SIRS or qSOFA

Empiric approach:

  • Unknown source → Tazocin 4.5 g IV q8h or Meropenem 1 g IV q8h (+ Vancomycin if indicated)
  • History/colonization with VRE → swap Vancomycin for Daptomycin 6 mg/kg IV q24h 

Bloodstream Infections:

  • Gram positive:
    • Vancomycin 1 g IV q12h OR daptomycin 6 mg/kg IV q24h if VRE history
    • Duration: 2 wks after negative cultures
    • If complicated (endocarditis, osteomyelitis): 4–6 wks
  • Gram negative:
    • Piptazo 4.5 g IV q8h OR meropenem 1 g IV q8h
    • Duration: 2 wks

Central Line Infection:

  • Remove line if:
    • Candida spp., Mycobacteria spp., S. aureus, S. lugdunensis, P. aeruginosa
    • Persistent positive cultures >72h
    • Signs of infection despite antibiotics
    • Complications: septic thrombophlebitis, endocarditis, osteomyelitis
    • Extensive cellulitis (>2 cm)
    • Relapsed infection post-treatment
  • If persistent after removal then investigate with ECHO, CT chest/abdo/pelvis

Intra-Abdominal Infection:

  • Work-up: CT abdomen/pelvis
  • Empiric:
    • Hx pseudomonas: Piptazo 4.5 g IV q8h or meropenem 1 g IV q8h + vancomycin 1 g IV q12h (daptomycin if VRE)
    • No pseudomonas: ertapenem 1 g IV q24h + vancomycin 1 g IV q12h (or dapto if VRE)

C. difficile:

  • Mild: vanco 125 mg PO QID × 10 days
  • Severe: vanco 500 mg PO QID + metronidazole 500 mg IV q8h + vanco 500 mg enema QID × 14 days
  • Recurrence:
    • 1st: vanco 125 mg PO QID × 10 days → taper regimen
    • 2nd: same + consider fecal transplant

Urinary Tract Infection (UTI)

  • Do not treat if asymptomatic and no recent kidney transplant (<2 months) or stent in place.
  • Empiric treatment:
  • Ceftriaxone 1 g IV q24h — 7 days for cystitis, 14–21 days for pyelonephritis.
  • If recent kidney transplant (<2 months) or stent in place: Ertapenem 1 g IV q24h + Daptomycin 6 mg/kg IV q24h if history of VRE.
  • Adjust antibiotics based on culture susceptibilities. 

  • Admit if: O₂ need, qSOFA+, multifocal pneumonia
  • Empiric:
    • CAP: ceftriaxone 1 g IV q24h + azithromycin 500 mg PO daily × 5–7 days
    • HCAP/VAP: Piptazo + azithromycin

Target organism: 

Candidemia

  • Remove central line
  • Daily blood cultures, ECHO, CT abdomen/pelvis, ophtho consult
  • Treatment:
    • On fluconazole prophylaxis/resistant: micafungin 100 mg IV daily
    • Otherwise: fluconazole 800 mg load, then 400 mg daily
  • Duration: 2 wks after negative cultures if no complications

Candiduria:

  • Do not treat if asymptomatic and no upcoming urological procedure.
  • If symptomatic → remove stent or exchange Foley catheter, order renal ultrasound to rule out abscess or fungal mass.
  • Treat with Fluconazole 400 mg IV/PO daily × 7–14 days, or Micafungin 100 mg IV daily × 7–14 days if resistant/allergic to fluconazole.

Histoplasma

  • Severe: amphotericin B 3 mg/kg/day × 2 wks
  • Then itraconazole 200 mg PO bid × 12 mo
  • Lifelong fluconazole 200 mg daily prophylaxis

Coccidioidomycosis

  • Severe: amphotericin B 5 mg/kg/day × 2 wks
    • Then fluconazole 400 mg PO bid × 12 mo
  • Lifelong fluconazole 200 mg daily prophylaxis

Blastomycosis

  • Severe: amphotericin B 5 mg/kg/day × 2 wks
  • Then itraconazole 200 mg PO bid × 12 mo
  • No long-term prophylaxis

Aspergillus

  • Voriconazole 6 mg/kg IV q12h × 1 day
    • Followed by 4 mg/kg IV q12h × 12 wks or until resolution 

CMV

  • Asymptomatic infection: valganciclovir 900 mg PO daily until PCR negative
  • CMV syndrome: valganciclovir 900 mg PO bid × 2–3 wks → daily × 3–8 wks until PCR negative for 2 wks
  • Invasive disease: ganciclovir 5 mg/kg IV bid × 2–3 wks → valganciclovir daily × 3–8 wks until PCR negative for 2 wks
  • Resistant CMV: foscarnet or cidofovir ± Cytogam; see mutation-specific guidance

HSV

  • Mucocutaneous: acyclovir 400 mg PO q8h × 5–7 days or until healed
  • Severe/CNS: acyclovir 10 mg/kg IV q8h × 3 wks
  • Keratitis: topical ganciclovir 0.15% Q2h ± acyclovir PO 

Toxoplasmosis

  • Induction: pyrimethamine 200 mg PO once → 75 mg PO daily + sulfadiazine 1.5 g PO q6h + leucovorin 25 mg PO daily × 6 wks
  • Maintenance: pyrimethamine 50 mg PO daily + sulfadiazine 4 g PO daily + leucovorin 25 mg PO daily × 12 mo
  • Lifelong Septra M/W/F prophylaxis 

Cellulitis

  • Uncomplicated (no abscess): Cephalexin 500 mg PO q6h × 5–7 days.
  • If MRSA suspected/confirmed: Septra DS PO BID × 5–7 days.
  • Complicated (abscess or requiring admission): Cefazolin 1 g IV q8h × 7–10 days OR Vancomycin 15 mg/kg IV q12h × 7–10 days.
  • If no improvement → consider Pseudomonas (ciprofloxacin PO or piptazo IV) or fungal cause (skin biopsy + fluconazole/micafungin).

Septic Arthritis

  • All cases require joint aspiration and orthopedic consult.
  • Empiric treatment: Ceftriaxone 2 g IV q24h OR Piptazo 4.5 g IV q8h + Vancomycin 15 mg/kg IV q12h.
  • Targeted therapy:
    • MSSA → Cefazolin 2 g IV q8h × 4 weeks.
    • MRSA → Vancomycin 15 mg/kg IV q12h × 4 weeks.
    • Gonorrhea → Ceftriaxone 2 g IV q24h × 7–10 days.

Osteomyelitis

  • Always obtain sterile deep tissue cultures (percutaneous or surgical) and involve ortho/wound care.
  • Empiric treatment:
    • Ceftriaxone 2 g IV q24h + Vancomycin 15 mg/kg q12h ± Metronidazole 500 mg q12h (if sacral involvement).
    • If ESBL history → Meropenem 1 g IV q8h + Vancomycin.
  • Targeted therapy:
    • MSSA → Cefazolin 2 g IV q8h × 6 weeks.
    • MRSA → Vancomycin 15 mg/kg IV q12h × 6 weeks.
    • E. coli/Klebsiella → Ceftriaxone 2 g IV q24h × 6 weeks.
    • ESBL → Meropenem 1 g IV q8h × 6 weeks.

Diabetic Foot Infection

  • Always assess vascular supply with ultrasound.
  • Mild infection (localized cellulitis <2 cm, superficial wound): Cephalexin 500 mg PO q6h × 7–14 days OR Septra DS BID × 7–14 days (if MRSA risk).
  • Moderate infection (cellulitis >2 cm, deeper involvement, purulence): Ceftriaxone 1 g IV q24h + Metronidazole 500 mg q12h ± Vancomycin (if MRSA risk).
  • Severe infection (necrosis, gangrene, hemodynamic instability, sepsis): Piptazo 4.5 g IV q8h OR Meropenem 1 g IV q8h (if ESBL) + Vancomycin 15 mg/kg q12h.

Meningitis / CNS Infections

  • Pre-LP Requirement: Always perform CT brain before lumbar puncture.
  • Empiric Treatment: Ceftriaxone 2 g IV q12h + Vancomycin (25 mg/kg IV loading, then 15 mg/kg q8h) + Ampicillin 2 g IV q4h + Acyclovir 10 mg/kg IV q8h.
  • Organism-Specific:
    • S. pneumoniae: Ceftriaxone ± Vancomycin (if penicillin-resistant) × 2 wks.
    • N. meningitidis: Ceftriaxone × 1 wk.
    • Listeria: Ampicillin × 3 wks, then Septra prophylaxis × 1 yr.
    • HSV: Acyclovir × 3 wks.
    • West Nile Virus: Ribavirin × 3 wks + IVIG × 5 days.
    • Invasive Fungal: Amphotericin B + Voriconazole until resolved.
    • Cryptococcus: Amphotericin B + Flucytosine × 2 wks → Fluconazole BID × 8 wks → Fluconazole daily × 12 mo.
    • Toxoplasmosis: Pyrimethamine + Sulfadiazine + Leucovorin × 6 wks → maintenance × 12 mo → Septra prophylaxis lifelong.

Endocarditis

  • Initial Steps: Obtain 3 sets of blood cultures 1 hr apart before starting antibiotics.
  • Empiric Treatment: Ceftriaxone 2 g IV q24h + Vancomycin 15 mg/kg IV q12h.
  • Organism-Specific:
    • MSSA:
      • Native: Cloxacillin or Cefazolin × 6 wks.
      • Prosthetic: Cloxacillin + Rifampin × 6 wks + Gentamicin × 2 wks.
    • MRSA:
      • Native: Vancomycin × 6 wks.
      • Prosthetic: Vancomycin + Rifampin × 6 wks + Gentamicin × 2 wks.
    • Strep viridans:
      • Native: Ceftriaxone × 4 wks.
      • Prosthetic: Ceftriaxone × 6 wks + Gentamicin × 2 wks.
    • Enterococci: Ampicillin + Ceftriaxone or Vancomycin × 6 wks.
    • HACEK: Ceftriaxone × 4 wks (native) or × 6 wks (prosthetic).
