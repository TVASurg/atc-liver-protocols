Immunosuppression Management:

Use of Sirolimus (3–8 ng/mL) may be considered in:

  • HCC (within Milan criteria)
  • CMV infection or reactivation
  • CNI neurotoxicity
  • Fibrosis
  • Chronic kidney disease
  • Suspected ductopenic rejection
  • Possibly ABMR

MASH Recipients:

  • Optimal IS: Myfortic (MYF) + low-dose tacrolimus (Tac)
  • Avoid sirolimus (increases steatosis)
  • Aim for steroid-free regimen
  • IS adjustment by MYF dose:
    • MYF 360–540 mg → reduce Tac target by 30%.
    • MYF 540–720 mg → reduce Tac target by 50%.

Notes for IS Management:

  • Alternatives to conventional tac:
    • Sublingual tacrolimus: For dysphagia. Reduce oral dose by 20%
    • Advagraf: Once-daily Tac formulation (no dose adjustment if coverage available)
    • Envarsus: Requires 30% lower dose than standard Tac
  • Steroids: High doses can lower Tac levels
    • Drug equivalence: 250 mg MMF = 180 mg EC-MPS (MYF).
  • Cyclosporine (CyA):
    • May be preferred for PBC post 12 months (lower recurrence risk with ursodiol 15 mg/kg).
    • Higher BPAR in first 12 months if used early.
  • Azathioprine (1–2 mg/kg) can replace MYF in patients with IBD.
  • Vedolizumab is the preferred biologic in IBD; avoid anti-TNF agents with Tac.
  • Patients on CyA for neurotoxicity: Re-trial Tac after 2–6 months if tolerated.
