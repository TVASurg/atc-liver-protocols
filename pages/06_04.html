Mayo Protocol for Hilar Cholangiocarcinoma:

Hilar cholangiocarcinoma often involves the bile duct at the liver hilum, making surgical resection difficult. This protocol uses neoadjuvant therapy before liver transplantation to control tumor growth, sterilize the tumor bed, and prevent recurrence.

When to Use:

  • Unresectable hilar cholangiocarcinoma < 3 cm
  • Diagnosis confirmed by:
    • Imaging (CT/MRI/MRCP) and
    • Pathology from brush cytology, biopsy, or FISH (aneuploidy)
    • OR CA 19-9 > 100 if histology not possible

Do NOT Use If (Exclusion Criteria)

  • Disease outside the hilum on imaging
  • Previous chemotherapy or radiation therapy
  • Uncontrolled infection
  • Major prior biliary surgery or resection attempt
  • Advanced local disease (vascular encasement, contralateral atrophy, extension below cystic duct)
  • Evidence of metastases on staging

Step-by-Step Protocol

1. Neoadjuvant External Beam Radiation

  • 45 Gy in 30 fractions (1.5 Gy twice daily over 3 weeks)
  • Continuous 5-fluorouracil (5-FU) infusion during radiation
  • Rationale: controls local disease and sensitizes tumor to radiation

2. Brachytherapy (2 weeks later)

  • 20 Gy at 1 cm depth over ~20â€“25 hours
  • Delivered directly to bile duct tumor via catheter
  • Rationale: delivers high dose to tumor while sparing surrounding tissue

3. Capecitabine Maintenance

  • Oral chemotherapy until transplant (pause during perioperative staging)
  • Rationale: continued suppression of microscopic disease

4. Pre-Transplant Staging Surgery

  • Laparotomy near transplant date (day before living donor transplant or before deceased donor offer)
  • Purpose: rule out occult metastases or peritoneal spread
  • If positive, transplant is aborted

5. Liver Transplant

  • Performed only if staging is negative for metastases or progression

Key Monitoring

  • Imaging at regular intervals to confirm no progression
  • Laboratory tumor markers (CA 19-9)
  • Toxicity monitoring for chemotherapy and radiation
