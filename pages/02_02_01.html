Contraindications to Listing:

1.   Co-Morbidities: Any medical condition reducing the estimated 5-year post-transplant survival below 60%. Unacceptably high peri-operative risk based on multidisciplinary assessment.

2.   Nutritional Status:

  •    BMI > 45 kg/m²: Absolute contraindication (except in exceptional circumstances).
  •    BMI > 40 kg/m²: Relative contraindication due to increased risk of morbidity/mortality.
  •    BMI < 18.5 kg/m²: Relative contraindication; aggressive nutritional support should be implemented.

3.   Infections

  •    Active or uncontrolled extrahepatic infection, including sepsis.
  •    Uncontrolled HIV (Detectable viral load and/or CD4 count < 100 cells/μL) or AIDS.

4.   Malignancy

  •    Presence of active extrahepatic malignancy.
  •    General requirement: 2–5 years cancer-free after curative treatment, depending on cancer type/stage.
  •    Individual assessment may be made for select malignancies.

5.   Vascular Abnormalities

  •    Extensive thrombosis or anomalies involving:
    •    Portal vein
    •    Superior mesenteric vein
    •    Splenic vein
  •    Any condition preventing adequate graft revascularization.

6.   Psychosocial Considerations

  •    Unstable psychiatric disorder that may interfere with adherence.
  •    Illicit drug misuse within the last 6 months.
  •    Documented or current non-adherence to medical advice.
  •    Inadequate social support to ensure post-operative medication compliance and follow-up care.
