HCV-Positive Recipients

  • Immunosuppression: Use higher Tacrolimus; avoid solumedrol boluses. Do not use depleting induction therapy.
  • Monitoring: Check HCV-RNA at week 2 post-transplant.

  • Antiviral Therapy: If RNA positive, start antiviral therapy (e.g., Epclusa or Maviret) beyond 3 months post-transplant.

    • May consider earlier treatment if active hepatitis; liver biopsy recommended to rule out TCMR

     

HBV-Positive Recipients

  •    If HBV-DNA negative at transplant: Life-long nucleos(t)ide analogs (NUCs). Step up LAM to tenofovir if necessary.
  •    If HBV-DNA positive at transplant: Use HBIG protocol + NUCs:
    • HBIG 10 mL IV during anhepatic phase, then:
      • Daily x1 week, weekly x4 weeks, monthly x12 months.
      • Maintain anti-HBs >100 IU/mL.
  •    If high HBV viral load (>4 logs):
    • Use High-Dose HBIG Protocol:
      • 10,000 IU IV at anhepatic phase.
      • 1500 IU/day Days 1–7, weekly x1 month, then monthly 1000–2000 IU.

      • Monitor anti-HBs titers (>100 IU/mL)

         

HBV/HDV Co-Infected Recipients

  • Therapy: Life-long HBIG + NUCs. Reinfection always leads to cirrhosis.

  • Goal: Maintain anti-HBs >50 IU/mL.

     

HEV-Positive Recipients

  •    Acute Liver Failure Triggered by HEV:
    • Avoid induction with basiliximab.
    • Consider IVIG during anhepatic phase (1.5 mg/kg).
    • If PCR-positive post-transplant with hepatitis, consider ribavirin, but note early infection risk.
