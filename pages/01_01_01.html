Since 1990, over 800 living liver donations have been successfully performed at Toronto General Hospital, with no donor deaths or long-term disabling complications. Becoming a donor requires excellent health, dedication, and a thorough screening process (only about 1 in 3 applicants qualify for surgery).

 

Living Donor Referral Process

All potential recipients undergoing evaluation for liver transplantation will receive the Pre-Liver Transplant Manual during their initial transplant clinic visit.

  • This manual outlines the transplant process, including the risks and benefits of living donation.
  • If the recipient is interested in pursuing living donation, the manual provides guidance on how to proceed.

Recipients who express interest in living donation will also be provided with the form titled “Evaluation of Living Liver Donors: Information for Liver Transplant Candidates and Caregivers” (LDL-001.SD04) which explains how living donors are evaluated. Members of the recipient team will review and discuss the details of the form with the candidate.

For more information refer to Living Donor Referral Process LDL-001

 

Living Donor Initial Contact and Information Process

The Living Donor Assessment Team does not initiate contact with potential donors. It is the responsibility of the interested individual to contact the Living Donor Office directly (via phone, email, in person, or through the UHN Transplant website). Once contact is made, the potential donor is provided with an information package, either through the website, email, mail, or in person.

This package includes:

  • Living Donor Liver Transplantation: Donor Information Manual (LDL-001.SD03)
  • Donor Health History Form (MOTDOC001)
  • Instructions for obtaining ABO blood type

The potential donor is asked to:

  1. Review the information manual
  2. Complete the health history form
  3. Obtain their ABO blood type
  4. Submit both the completed form and ABO documentation to the Living Donor Assessment Office

To begin the process, they must complete a Donor Health History Form and submit it with proof of blood type to the Living Donor Liver Office. Forms are available at: www.uhn.ca/Transplant/Liver_Transplant_Program

 

Process

1. Donor Health History Review and Initial Evaluation Process:

  • Upon receiving the completed Donor Health History Form and ABO Blood Type, the Living Donor Coordinator and Administrative Assistant will:   

    • Review the Health History Form for completeness
    • Calculate the donor’s Body Mass Index (BMI)
    • Log the date the Health History Form and ABO blood type were receive
    • Generate a Medical Record Number (MRN) in the EPR system
    • Create a referral in OTTR and link the donor to the intended recipient (if applicable)
    • Evaluate donor suitability for assessment; if needed, escalate to the Medical Director/designate for input

    Note: Forms are typically reviewed within 2 business days of receipt.

     

2. Notification and Pre-Assessment Coordination:

  • If the donor is deemed suitable for assessment: The Living Donor Coordinator informs the recipient team of a potential donor and awaits confirmation to proceed.
  • If the recipient is not yet listed, both teams must discuss and may initiate preliminary donor assessment if reasonable.

3. Donor Workup Initiation

  • The Living Donor Coordinator schedules initial donor appointments.
  • One of the first appointments is with a Living Donor Physician or Surgeon, accompanied by the Coordinator.
  • The donor will be instructed to review and sign the Donor Assessment Consent:
    • Consent to be Assessed for Living Liver Donation LDL-002.SD01 (for Residents of Canada)
    • Consent to be Assessed for Living Liver Donation non-resident LDL-002.SD02 (for Non-Residents)

4. Donor Candidate Prioritization and Selection

  • Only one donor is evaluated at a time (in rare cases, two may be assessed).
  • All potential donors are reviewed and ranked. If needed, ranking is confirmed with the Medical Director/designate.
  • Once the lead candidate is selected:
    • They are informed by the Living Donor Team

    • The Administrative Assistant initiates a paper chart and registers the donor in OTTR and EPR

The following information must be recorded in the donor’s medical chart:

  • Donor’s full name and address
  • Medical Record Number (MRN)
  • Date of birth
  • ABO blood type
  • Signed Documentation of the Decision to be Assessed form:
  • LDL-002.SD01 (Residents) or LDL-002.SD02 (Non-Residents)
  • Name of the healthcare professional who reviewed the Health History Form
  • Any available medical records 

The Living Donor Liver Informed Consent (LDL-002) outlines that all communication with the Living Donor Assessment Team is confidential, and personal health information will only be shared with the recipient if the donor provides specific written consent.  

The informed consent process for living liver donation ensures that potential donors fully understand the medical, surgical, psychosocial, and logistical aspects of donation.  

Discussions include:  

  1. Details of the assessment process, including medical evaluations, lab testing, and the ability to stop the process at any time due to medical, psychological, or recipient-related concerns
  2. Surgical risks, the possibility of intraoperative cancellation, post-operative care, and recovery expectations
  3. Alternative treatments available to the recipient
  4. Potential risks to the donor, such as infection, bleeding, organ failure, depression, anxiety, or guilt
  5. Transplant program outcomes, including survival rates and the rare possibility that the donor may one day need a transplant themselves
  6. Health insurance and financial implications, such as limited future coverage and access to reimbursement through the PRELOD program
  7. Donor’s absolute right to withdraw at any point, with confidentiality maintained (only their unsuitability (not the reason) is shared with the recipient).

Donors must consent to the release or receipt of medical records via the appropriate authorization form and sign a separate consent for email communication. Formal consent to be assessed is obtained by the coordinator using standardized forms for residents or non-residents, ensuring the donor understands the risks, alternatives, and right to opt out. Surgical consent is completed by the surgeon during consultation and includes authorization for blood product use. All signed documents are recorded in the donor’s chart or EPR.
