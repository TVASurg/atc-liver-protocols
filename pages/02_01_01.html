Indications:

 

Following list provides conditions that may require a liver transplant:

 

1.   Chronic Liver Disease (Cirrhosis-related)

  •    Hepatitis C (HCV)
  •    Hepatitis B (HBV)
  •    Alcohol-associated liver disease (ALD)
  •    Non-alcoholic steatohepatitis (NASH)
  •    Autoimmune hepatitis
  •    Primary biliary cholangitis (PBC)

  •    Primary sclerosing cholangitis (PSC)

2.   Acute Liver Failure

  •    Acetaminophen toxicity (most common cause)
  •    Acute autoimmune hepatitis
  •    Acute viral hepatitis

  •    Wilson’s disease (acute hepatic failure presentation)

3.   Liver Malignancies

  •    Hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC) (within Milan or expanded criteria)
  •    Cholangiocarcinoma (in select cases under protocol)

4.   Genetic / Pediatric / Congenital Conditions

  •    Biliary atresia (most common pediatric indication)
  •    Alpha-1 antitrypsin deficiency
  •    Wilson’s disease
  •    Hemochromatosis
  •    Glycogen storage diseases (GSD)
  •    Progressive familial intrahepatic cholestasis (PFIC)

  •    Crigler-Najjar syndrome

5.   Vascular & Structural Liver Disorders

  •    Budd-Chiari syndrome
  •    Polycystic liver disease
  •    Portal vein thrombosis (in context of liver failure)
  •    Hyperoxaluria (often in combined liver-kidney transplant)
