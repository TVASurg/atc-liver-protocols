Listing and Waitlist Management (SAH):

Following is for patients experiencing their first episode of liver decompensation due to alcohol and who may not meet traditional 6-month abstinence requirements.

Eligibility Criteria:

  • Medical Inclusion Criteria:
    • First decompensating liver event  
    • Maddrey’s Discriminant Function ≥32.  
    • Lille Model score ≥0.45 after 7 days of corticosteroids, increasing MELD score while on therapy, or contraindication to steroid therapy are all acceptable indicators.
    • Diagnosis must be confirmed by liver biopsy.  
    • No absolute contraindications to transplantation.
  • Psychosocial Inclusion Criteria:
    • Willingness and capacity to abstain from alcohol
    • Engage in addiction treatment pre- and post-transplant
    • No more than one previously failed alcohol use disorder (AUD) treatment
    • They should not have any active substance use disorder (except cannabis or tobacco), or untreated, severe psychiatric illness that would interfere with treatment adherence.  
    • Reliable contact, stable housing, and a dedicated support person are mandatory. 
Evaluation Process:
Stage  Tasks 

Referral Phase  

(Days 1–11) 

  • Referring physicians assess clinical severity using Maddrey DF and initiate steroid therapy.  
  • At Day 7, the Lille Model score is calculated to evaluate response.  
  • Patients meeting criteria are referred to the ALD program, and a liver biopsy is arranged.  
  • A completed referral form and psychosocial questionnaire must be submitted. 

Consultation Phase  

(Days 12–14) 

  • Addiction Psychiatry and Social Work teams assess the patient’s psychosocial suitability, including alcohol use testing with EtG.  
  • Patients capable of consenting sign an agreement to remain abstinent & engage in treatment.  
  • If deemed eligible, a medical assessment follows. All findings are reviewed by the transplant listing committee, and a listing decision is made 
Waitlist Management 
  • Patients accepted into the program will be monitored monthly for clinical stability and alcohol use, including Na MELD scoring, EtG or CDT testing, and monthly addiction and transplant team check-ins.
  • Failure to adhere to medical/addiction follow-up may result in the patient being placed on hold or removed from the waitlist.  
  • First positive alcohol test results in hold; a second leads to removal.  
  • Patients may also be removed for loss of follow-up, poor psychosocial support, or voluntary withdrawal from transplant consideration. 

Post-Transplant Follow-Up: Patients will continue relapse prevention therapy and undergo random EtG or CDT testing in the first year post-transplant. Annual medical and psychosocial assessments will be conducted, with toolkits and support provided to community providers.

Tools and Assessment Instruments:

  • Biomarkers: EtG (urine), CDT (blood)
  • Psychosocial Tools: SIPAT (Stanford Integrated Psychosocial Assessment for Transplant), HRAR (High Risk Alcohol Relapse), ARRA (Alcohol Relapse Risk Assessment)
