Pre-Operative (Donor):

Surgery Day Overview: 

Time  Activity 
6:00 am 

Donor arrives at Surgical Admissions (Norman Urquhart Wing).

  • No food or drink after midnight.
  • Bring health card and medications
  • Support person(s) may accompany
  • Change into hospital gown; IV line inserted
  • Nurses administer antibiotics, anti-clot injections, and compression stockings.
  • Anesthesia team discusses pain management; arterial line placement discussed.
  • Surgeon marks surgical site. 
8:00 am  Donor enters the Operating Room (OR) for a 6–8-hour procedure. 
10:00–11:00 am  Recipient enters OR holding area. If donor cholangiogram is satisfactory, surgery proceeds. 
3:00–5:00 pm 

Donor surgery finishes, recovery room stay for 2–3 hours.

  • Breathing tube removed, pain management begins.
  • Transfer to Step-Down Unit (10th Floor).
  • Surgeon updates donor family. 
6:00–7:00 pm  Donor admitted to Step-Down Unit (ACU). Visitors allowed after 30-minute assessment. 1–2 visitors at a time. 
7:00–8:00 pm  Recipient surgery ends. Recipient transferred to ICU or ACU. Surgeon updates recipient family. Brief visits allowed with recipient in ICU. 

Lines and Tubes Used During Donor Surgery:

  • IV Catheter: Inserted pre-op for medications and anesthesia.
  • Arterial Line: Inserted (usually post-sedation) in the wrist to monitor blood pressure.
  • Nasogastric Tube: Inserted through the nose into the stomach to prevent post-op nausea.
  • Central Line: Inserted in a neck/chest vein for fluids, meds, and blood draws.
  • Foley Catheter: Drains bladder during and after surgery to monitor urine output.

For more information as well as days following the surgery review Living Donor Liver Pre-Operative Information (Document LDL-003.SD03)
