Medications (Bacterial Prophylaxis):

Septra / Dapsone / Atovaquone / Pentamidine:

  • Indicated for all patients for PJP prophylaxis.
  • Start at 1-month post-LT (or 2 weeks if received ATG), continue until 1 year.
  • Lifelong prophylaxis for patients with HIV or toxoplasmosis.
  • Dose:
    • Septra: 1 SS tab PO Mon/Wed/Fri.
    • If allergic/side effects (pancytopenia, high LFT, GI upset) to Septra: Atovaquone at1500 mg PO daily
    • If allergy or GI upset to above: Pentamidine aerosolized 300 mg every 4 weeks
    • Alternative: Dapsone 50–100 mg PO daily (requires G6PD screening).

Rifampin / Isoniazid / Rifapentine

  • Indicated for latent TB or high TB risk (positive TST/IGRA, history of untreated TB, contact exposure, donor with latent TB, radiologic evidence)
  • Dose:
    • Rifampin: 10 mg/kg PO daily (max 600 mg) × 4 months.
    • If allergic/intolerant:
      • Isoniazid: 5 mg/kg (max 300 mg) PO daily × 6–9 months
      • Rifapentine: 900 mg weekly (>50 kg) × 4 months.
      • Isoniazid + Rifapentine: same doses × 3 months.
    • Stop if liver enzymes >5× ULN (or >3× ULN with symptoms).
    • Add Pyridoxine (B6) 50 mg to all regimens containing isoniazid.
