INDILIV (Individualized Immunosuppression after Liver Transplant) Beyond Year 1:

Footnotes:

  1. Inflammation assessed via histology (BANFF-mini): mild portal inflammation with no interface, vascular, or biliary changes.
  2. Consider switching to sirolimus 3–8 ng/mL if covered and fibrosis >F2 but not in MASH (metabolic-associated steatohepatitis).
  3. Consider adding steroids if no histologic improvement is observed.
  4. Patients with moderate/severe early TCMR (T-cell mediated rejection) or late TCMR (>3 months post-transplant) are at medium to high risk and should be managed cautiously after biopsy.

Routine Follow-Up Schedule (Years 1, 3, 5):

  • Bloodwork: ALT, AST, ALP, INR, bilirubin, creatinine (GFR), LDL, HbA1c
  • Urine: Albumin-to-creatinine ratio (UACR)
  • HBV serology if transplanted for HBV
  • Fibroscan
  • Liver biopsy:
    • Strongly recommended at Years 1, 3, 5 with DSAs.  
    • If biopsy timing differs, adjust immunosuppression (IS) by only one step at a time.
    • Expected findings from protocol biopsies:
      • Low risk: 20%
      • Medium risk: 50%
      • High risk: 30%

Biopsy should always be obtained:

  • Before reducing IS to monotherapy or CNI-free regimens.
  • 1–2 years after major IS reduction.
  • With ALT elevation and DSA positivity
  • de novo DSA.
  • After moderate/severe early or late TCMR (>3 months)
  • If ALT remains elevated on multiple occasions
  • If liver stiffness ≥7kPa or DSA present.

Safety notes:

  • Protocol biopsies are safe unless intrahepatic bile ducts are dilated.
  • Rule out bile duct dilatation with pre-biopsy ultrasound, especially with ALP elevation or prior biliary strictures.
  • LDLT patients have a higher likelihood of ductal dilatation and mobile grafts—maintain low threshold for ultrasound.
