Donor Selection and Factors

Deceased donors are assessed based on various clinical and histological factors. Patients undergoing liver transplantation for fulminant hepatic failure or re-transplantation should ideally receive grafts with one or no abnormal donor factors. 

Donor Factors    Details
Age  
  1.     < 55 (Use).
  2.    55-60 (Use if only one other donor factor abnormal).
  3.    > 60 (only Use if no other risk factors present). 
Macro steatosis   
  1.    Macro steatosis < 30% (Use)  
  2.    Macro steatosis 30-60% (Only Use if no other risk factor present)  
  3.    Macro steatosis  > 60% (Never Use)  
HBV  

 1. Donors with HBsAg + with minimal fibrosis and no focal lesions are considered Use in recipients with HBsAg + ( will need HBV anti-viral).  

  • Don’t Use in patient in HDV positive recipients.

2. Anti-HBc positive +

  • Use in recipients with HBsAg + recipients (will need HBV anti-viral).  
  • Use in recipients with HBsAb + and  HBcAb + if previously consented (will not need HBV anti-viral).  
  • Use in recipients with HBsAb - and  HBcAb + if previously consented (will need HBV anti-viral).
  • Use in HBV naïve recipients if previously consented (will need HBV anti-viral). 
HCV   Can Use HCV + donors in all patients if they agree and its documents before-hand.  
Infections  

Contraindicated: HIV+, active bacteremia/fungemia, active TB, WNV+, HTLV+  

Acceptable with precautions: Syphilis+ (treat recipient), CMV+, EBV+, toxoplasma+  

Malignancy  

Patients with basal cell carcinoma or squamous cell skin cancer or a CNS cancer (other than medulloblastoma with no VP shunt) OR papillary thyroid cancer < 2 cm then Use.  

Don’t Use All other cancers.  

Liver disease  

Any known cirrhosis then Don’t Use  

If LFTs are elevated (less than 3-time ULL) with no known liver disease and its thought to be related to shocked liver then Use.

If LFTs are elevated ( 3-5 time ULL) with no known liver disease and its thought to be related to shocked liver then Use if only one other donor factor abnormal.

If LFTS are elevated ( more than 5 times ULL) then don’t Use. 

Na level  

If Na is <160 then Use.

If Na is >160 then  Use if only one other donor factor abnormal.  

Size match on imaging    Surgeon need to review the images.  
Cold ischemic time  

<12 hours then Use.

12-14 hours then Use if not DCD donor.  

>14 hours then don’t Use. 

Organox Consider Organox for DCD livers or marginal livers.
