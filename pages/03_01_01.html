Listing and Waitlist Management (HCC):

Review LIVa-006.SD01 for full details on the clinical, regulatory, and institutional standards for evaluating HCC patients for liver transplant candidacy at the Ajmera Transplant Centre.

  • All referrals for HCC must follow the Liver Transplant Referral Process (LIVa-001).
  • Within TGLN criteria: Patients seen by a transplant hepatologist within 2 weeks.
  • Outside TGLN criteria: Redirected to Surgical Oncology, with potential re-referral at their discretion.
  • Surgical oncology is responsible for imaging, bridging therapy, and presenting at HCC/HPB rounds.
  • Decisions are documented in EPIC.

Eligibility Criteria:

  • Meet minimum eligibility as outlined in SOP LIVa-002 and LIVa-003
  • The disease trajectory must justify transplant compared to expected survival post-transplant
  • All alternative treatments must be considered prior to referral.
  • Deceased Donor (Ontario Criteria):
    • Total Tumor Volume (TTV) ≤ 145 cm³ and AFP < 1000 ng/mL (TTV = 4/3 πr³)
    • Single tumors <2 cm successfully treated with ablation/resection are not eligible for exception points.
    • Recurrent HCC within criteria may be eligible for exception points if recurrence occurs within 5 years.
  • Living Donor (Extended Toronto Criteria):
    • TTV > 145 cm³ allowed if AFP < 1000 ng/mL, and No cancer symptoms/No extrahepatic disease or vascular invasion/No poor tumor differentiation on biopsy
    • If AFP > 1000 ng/mL, downstaging via locoregional therapy is required.

Contraindications (Pre-Transplant):

  • Absolute
    • Any contraindication in SOP LIVa-002
    • Extrahepatic spread or vascular invasion unless part of a research protocol
    • Mixed HCC/cholangiocarcinoma histology
  • Relative
    • Case-by-case review by transplant team if any relative contraindications are present (see SOP LIVa-002)

Waitlist Monitoring:

All listed HCC candidates will undergo regular surveillance to ensure continued eligibility including:

  • CT chest/abdomen/pelvis every 3 months
  • AFP measurement every 3 months
  • Bridging therapies documented and organized by surgical oncology
  • Exception points may be granted during monitoring

Immune Checkpoint Inhibitor (ICI) Considerations

  • All HCC patients beyond Milan criteria should be considered for ICI-based therapy.
  • Patients on ICI will be placed on hold once MELD reaches 30 exception points.
  • ICI is discontinued at MELD 30, and patients are re-activated after a 6-week washout period.
  • Bridging options discussed at HCC/HPB rounds
