Initial Workup 

Work-up type   Details  
Basic bloodwork   CBC, lytes, Ca, creat, LFT, Bil, Albumin, INR, lipid profile, TSH, urine B-HCG 
Infection screening  
  1. Infections: HIV, HBV, HCV, CMV, EBV, syphilis TP-EIA, toxoplasmosis, TB skin test, blood culture.  
  2. Specific infections if endemic: Strongyloidiasis, WNV, Trypanosoma, Coccidioides, Histoplasma. 
Screening for liver disease   Iron profile, ceruloplasmin, alpha1 antitrypsin, AMA, SMA, ANA.  
Cardiac testing 
  1. ECG + ECHO for all  
  2. Stress testing: MIBI, DSE or CT coronary if age >50, ECG is abnormal, or if pre-existing cardiac history.  
Pulmonary testing  
  1. CXR for all.
  2. PFT if current or previous smoker or if history of asthma. 
Imaging   Triphasic CT with 3D reconstruction and MRI/ MRCP for all. 
Consultations  
  1. Psychiatry, General medicine, anesthesia, surgery and SW for all  
  2. Other consults PRN  

During the screening process, any issues that arise should be documented using the form titled

“Living Liver Donor – Issues Identified During Work-Up Tracking Tool (LDL-003.SD01).”

 

Following all of the tests, the following is considered when selecting potential living donors: 

Factors   Details  
Age   Anyone between 16-60 
Medical illness   Any major chronic medical illness is a contraindicated ( except mild HTN or dyslipidemia).   
Infection   Any infection is a contraindication unless treated or controlled. 
Prior surgery  

Prior liver donation or any major abdominal surgeries ( gastric bypass or whipple) is a contraindication.  

Minor abdominal surgeries (cholecystectomy or appendectomy) are acceptable.  

Surgeries done to other parts of the body are acceptable.  

Psychological illness   Any major psychological illness or Social situation that would make if difficult to get an informed consent or if there is possible pressure or financial gain is a contraindication.  
Liver disease   Any liver disease is a contraindication.  
Steatosis/ BMI   

All patients with BMI above 30 need to lose weight.

Steatosis <10% is acceptable.

Steatosis between 10-30 need to lose weight and possibly confirmed by liver biopsy.  

Steatosis >30% is a contraindication. 

Imaging  

Donor remnant liver volume should be >30%  

GRBW should be >0.8%.

Graft should not be too big for the recipient.  

Once all investigations have been completed and the donor is deemed suitable for liver donation, the Living Donor Physician should complete the “Living Liver Donor Assessment Summary” form (LDL-006.SD01).
