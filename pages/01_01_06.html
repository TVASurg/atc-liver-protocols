Hepatitis Virus Positive Donors:

  • HCV-Positive Donors
    • Transmission Risk: Low with NAT-negative donors.
    • Pre-Transplant Biopsy: Required to rule out fibrosis ≥F2.
    • Post-Transplant Monitoring: HCV-RNA at weeks 2 and 12.
    • Antiviral Therapy: If RNA positive at both time points, initiate therapy beyond 3 months (Epclusa or Maviret for 12 weeks).
  • HBV-Positive Donors: Pre-Transplant Biopsy: Required to assess for fibrosis ≥F2
  • HCC Surveillance: Annual liver ultrasound and AFP.

 

Donor Profiles:

1. anti-HBc+, HBsAg- Donors:

  • Preferred for older recipients.
  • No HBIG needed.
  • Follow NUC-based prophylaxis:
    • anti-HBc+ recipient: Start NUCs; may wean in anti-HBs+ recipients.
    • Vaccinated recipient (anti-HBs+ only): Start NUCs; may wean if titers stable.
    • Seronegative recipients: Start NUCs; vaccinate after 3–6 months.

2. HBsAg+ Donors: Not currently allocated by TGLN.

3. HBV/HDV+ Donors: Not accepted due to high risk of early refibrosis.
