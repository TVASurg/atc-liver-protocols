Post-operative (Living Donor)

Post-op goals are the following:

  • Prevent or detect post-operative complications early
  • Monitor for signs of liver failure
  • Ensure long-term follow-up and support

Immediate Post-Operative Care (Inpatient Phase):

  • All post-operative donors are admitted to the Transplant Acute Care Unit (ACU) or Intensive Care Unit (ICU) after PACU discharge.
  • Care is guided by the ACU/Floor Post-Operative Liver Donor Orders (Form D-2639).
  • Donors will be on cardiac monitoring, with arterial and central lines in place.
  • Transfer to the Inpatient Transplant Unit occurs once transfer criteria from Form D-2639 are met.

Discharge Instructions:

  • Donors are typically discharged on post-operative day 5–7.
  • Prior to discharge:
    • Self-injection with Tinzaparin is taught
    • Post-Discharge Care Guidelines (LDL-008.SD01) are provided and reviewed
    • The Inpatient Discharge Coordinator reviews instructions with the donor and their family 
Time Frame Follow-Up Requirements 
10 days – 2 weeks 
  • Post-op phone call from Living Donor Coordinator to assess recovery and answer questions  
  • Tests if clinically indicated (LFTs, liver ultrasound, etc.) 
1 month 
  • Surgeon visit
  • Bloodwork (LFTs and CBC)
  • Physical exam or virtual consult
  • Review with coordinator
  • Liver ultrasound if needed 
3 months 
  • Bloodwork (LFTs)
  • Abdominal MRI or ultrasound if available
  • Results forwarded to GP 
1–10 years  Annual physical with GP including labs for liver function
